Buying guide â€” how to choose Chiniot furniture

Choosing the right piece requires attention to materials, construction, finish, and ergonomics. Below is a checklist to help:

  • Inspect joinery: Solid joints (not just nails) are a sign of quality.
  • Check the wood: Look for consistent grain and properly seasoned timber (less cracking).
  • Ask about finishes: Natural oils (like tung or linseed) vs. polyurethane â€” oils are easier to repair; poly is tougher.
  • Request measurements: Always measure your space and doorways for delivery.
  • Warranty & support: A trustworthy maker will offer minor repairs or advice post-sale.

Care & maintenance

To keep Chiniot furniture looking its best:

  • Dust regularly with a soft cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners.
  • Use coasters and placemats to protect surfaces from heat & moisture.
  • Keep away from direct sunlight and extreme humidity to prevent warping.
  • Refresh natural oil finishes annually; polish lacquer finishes when needed.
